# Jeevika Prototype Research Notes

## Build lens

This prototype is intentionally optimized for judging clarity and real-world relevance, not for full content breadth. The core flow is:

1. Separate home and business money
2. Protect against emergency shock
3. Resist digital fraud under pressure
4. End with a practical action list

## Cross-validated pain points

### 1. Women still face a serious mobile access and usage gap

GSMA's 2025 South Asia analysis notes that women in South Asia are significantly less likely than men to use mobile internet. That matters because any financial-literacy product here must be simple, low-bandwidth, and forgiving.

Source:
- https://www.gsma.com/solutions-and-impact/connectivity-for-good/mobile-for-development/country/india/the-mobile-gender-gap-in-south-asia-is-now-widening/

### 2. Access to accounts does not mean confident digital usage

Current industry reporting around women and inclusion in India keeps pointing to low trust in fully digital journeys and a strong preference for in-person interaction. That supports a prototype that builds confidence gradually instead of assuming digital fluency.

Source used in product reasoning:
- https://www.dbs.com/newsroom/

### 3. Fraud prevention must be part of the product, not an afterthought

India's cyber-fraud reporting flow emphasizes speed, especially the 1930 helpline and reporting through the national cybercrime portal. The prototype therefore includes an OTP trap as a core mechanic rather than a side note.

Sources:
- https://www.cybercrime.gov.in/
- https://www.mha.gov.in/en/divisionofmha/cyber-and-information-security-cis-division

### 4. Payment safety should be explained in plain behavior, not jargon

UPI safety messaging across official ecosystems consistently reinforces that credentials, OTPs, and PINs should not be shared. The prototype turns that into a pressured choice moment.

Source:
- https://www.npci.org.in/what-we-do/upi/product-overview

## Product consequence of this research

- The interface is mobile-first
- Hindi is foregrounded
- Touch targets are large
- The emotional arc is more important than deep feature count
- The prototype focuses on behavior change, not quiz completion
- Government schemes appear as choices with consequences, not static information
